## KQL 301: Section 1: Introduction<br>
### **Summary**<br>
Real investigations don't happen in a single table.
<br><br>
When you're tracking an attack, the pieces are scattered across different data sources. The email logs show who received a phishing message. The network logs reveal who clicked the link. The authentication logs show whether anyone entered their credentials. To understand what really happened, you need to connect these tables together.
<br><br>
In KQL 301, you'll learn how to combine data from multiple tables using joins and unions. You'll also pick up advanced techniques for reshaping data, like expanding lists into individual rows and extracting structured information from messy strings.
<br><br>
We'll put these skills to work investigating a phishing attack from the first email through to the attacker's final actions.
